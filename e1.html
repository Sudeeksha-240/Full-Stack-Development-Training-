Brief History of Modern India


Modern India, generally considered to span from the mid-18th century to the present, is characterized by the decline of the Mughal empire, the rise of British colonial rule, the Indian independence movement, and the subsequent establishment of a parliamentary democracy. This period includes significant social, economic, and political transformations shaped by both internal dynamics and external influences.
Decline of the Mughal Empire: The weakening Mughal rule created a power vacuum that allowed European trading companies, particularly the British East India Company, to establish their dominance.
